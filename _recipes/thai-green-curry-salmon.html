--- layout: recipe image_src: /assets/images/recipes/Thai-Green-Curry-Salmon.jpg image_alt: Thai Green Curry Salmon description: > Ready to lick your plate? The medley of vegetables paired with the creaminess of the curry and lightness of the salmon is sure to leave you wanting more. total_time: 1 Hour servings: 3–4 skill_level: Easy oven_temperature: 450 ingredients: - 4 T. olive oil - 1 zucchini, halfed and mooned - 2 serrano peppers, seeds removed and chopped - 2 cloves of garlic, minced - 5 baby bella mushrooms, remove stem and slice - 1/2 red bell pepper, seeds removed and chopped - 1 head of baby bok choy, chopped - 1 in. piece of ginger, peeled and minced - 3–4 green onions, chopped - 1 lb. salmon - Montreal steak seasoning (can substitue with salt and pepper) - 13.5 oz. coconut milk - 2 T. Thai green curry paste - 1 T. fish sauce - 2 T. lime juice - 1/4 cup fresh basil, chopped instructions: - Preheat oven to 450º. - Cut all ingredients so that they are ready to go before the salmon bakes. - On a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil place the salmon skin side down. Drizzle the salmon with 2 T. of olive oil, season with Montreal steak seasoning. - Bake salmon for 12 to 15 minutes or until salmon is cooked through. - While salmon is baking, heat a large skillet with 2 T. of olive oil. - When oil is hot, add zucchini, serrano peppers, garlic, mushrooms, red pepper, bok choy, ginger, and the white part of the green onions. Saute for around 4 minutes or until vegetables become tender. - Stir in the green curry paste, saute for 1 to 2 minutes. - Turn the heat to medium-low and add coconut milk, fish sauce, lime juice, and the rest of the green onion. Simmer, stirring occasionally, for about 5-10 minutes or until salmon is done cooking. - Remove the salmon from the oven and with a stiff spatula, remove the skin. - Serve the salmon over the curry and top with freshly chopped basil. tags: - Create & Eat - Dinner - Salmon - Thai - Curry ---
